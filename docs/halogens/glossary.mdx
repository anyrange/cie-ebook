## Glossary

<p>
  <strong>bleach</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/282.5936b906.mp3' /> -  NaClO
</p>
<p>
  <strong>chlorate ion</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/283.63fdbd43.mp3' /> – ClO-
</p>
<p>
  <strong>chloric (I) acid</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/284.24249907.mp3' /> - HClO
</p>
<p>
  <strong>diatomic</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/285.5f4fb4b5.mp3' /> – molecules that exist as two atoms covalently bonded together.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>displacement</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/286.c741becc.mp3' /> – the reaction when a more reactive halogen reacts with a compound containing a less reactive halogen.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>halide</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/287.cc56fa62.mp3' /> –  the name of a halogen when it has reacted with another substance and gained a full outer electron shell.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>halogen</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/288.7d921b4a.mp3' /> – an element that belongs to group 7 of the periodic table.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>hydrogen halide</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/289.f1d89284.mp3' /> – a compound formed from the reaction between hydrogen and a halogen.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>metal halide</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/290.b916830d.mp3' /> –  a compound formed from the reaction between a metal and a halogen.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>sublime</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/291.e72b8773.mp3' /> -  to change from a solid to a gas without first becoming a liquid.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>volatile</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/292.5d5c806a.mp3' /> – a substance that evaporates or produces vapour at relatively low temperatures.
</p>
