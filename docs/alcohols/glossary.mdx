## Glossary

<p>
  <strong>alcohol</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/175.eec02e69.mp3' /> - a family of compounds which contains the –OH group.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>aldehyde</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/176.fef477b1.mp3' /> – primary alcohol oxidation product.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>anaerobic</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/177.9200f26a.mp3' /> - reaction which takes place without oxygen.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>biofuel</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/178.2a105952.mp3' /> – a fuel derived or produced from renewable biological sources.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>carbon-neutral</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/179.052de9ac.mp3' /> – biofuel does not have net carbon dioxide emissions during the process from growing to combustion.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>chain reaction</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/180.3692b6d7.mp3' /> – a series of reactions where the products of one reaction are the reactants for the next.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>chlorofluorocarbon</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/181.65ab38e6.mp3' /> – a compound made of chlorine, fluorine and carbon only.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>dehydration</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/182.161b468a.mp3' /> – the name of the reaction when alcohols undergo elimination to give alkenes, a molecule of water is removed.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>ethanol</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/183.e3b3b60a.mp3' /> – the alcohol which contains two carbon atoms. Formula: CH<sub>3</sub>CH<sub>2</sub>OH
</p>
<p>
  <strong>ester</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/184.b367325b.mp3' /> -  a family of compound which contain the group –COO–
</p>
<p>
  <strong>fermentation</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/185.959cfe52.mp3' /> – reaction in which yeast is used to convert glucose into ethanol.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>free radical</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/186.880db705.mp3' /> – a highly reactive substance which contains a single unpaired electron.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>hydrofluorocarbon</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/187.c45dc9fe.mp3' /> – a compound made of hydrogen, fluorine and carbon only.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>inert</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/188.d0448ebc.mp3' /> – very unreactive.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>intermolecular forces</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/189.0733a446.mp3' /> – the forces which hold molecules together.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>ketone</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/190.4c938e7a.mp3' /> – secondary alcohol oxidation product.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>methanol</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/191.71f08191.mp3' /> – the simplest alcohol, containing only one carbon. Formula: CH<sub>3</sub>OH.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>ozone</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/192.d41e42e6.mp3' /> – a form of oxygen with the formula O<sub>3</sub>
</p>
<p>
  <strong>ozone layer</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/193.1060bb2a.mp3' /> – a layer of the stratosphere, which contains high levels of ozone.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>primary alcohol</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/194.a1c42fc3.mp3' /> – the carbon atom where the -OH group is only attached to one alkyl group.atoms
</p>
<p>
  <strong>secondary alcohol</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/195.4c475e4c.mp3' /> – the carbon atom tha carries the -OH group is attached to two alkyl group
</p>
<p>
  <strong>stratosphere</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/196.bc29e487.mp3' /> – layer of the Earth’s atmosphere, 15-30 km above the Earth’s surface.
</p>
<p>
  <strong>ultraviolet</strong>
  <AudioButton url='/media/197.0a86bf16.mp3' /> – a type of electromagnetic radiation.
</p>
