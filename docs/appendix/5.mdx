### <center>In the Chemistry Laboratory</center>

#### Safety precautions:

<table>
  <tr>
    <td>Avoid inhaling vapours.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Avoid skin contact with the reactants and products. Wear gloves.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>
      Ensure burettes are filled when the top of the burette is below eye level.
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Ensure the delivery tube does not become blocked.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Ethanoic acid will sting if it gets into cuts in the skin.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>
      Pay attention to the hazard warnings on the tubs and bottles of unknown
      substances. In particular, if anything is marked flammable, do not use
      anywhere near a lit Bunsen burner. If heating a flammable substance is
      required, then it must be heated by standing it in a beaker of hot water.
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Phenol is corrosive and toxic.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Potassium nitrate is oxidizing.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Sulfuric acid is an irritant.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>The product of the distillation process is flammable.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>This procedure requires the use of a working fume cupboard.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Tie long hair back.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Unknown inorganic solids must be regarded as harmful.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Unknown organic liquids must be treated as harmful and flammable.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Wear a lab coat,gloves and use eye protection.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Zinc sulfate is harmful.</td>
  </tr>
</table>
