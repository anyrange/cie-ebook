# Language structure

## Cause and Effect relationships

<dl>
  <dt>
    <b>+ Advantage</b>
  </dt>
  <dd>
    <em>e.g.</em> There are several <u>advantages of</u> catalytic cracking over
    thermal cracking.
  </dd>
  <dt>
    <b>+ Benefit</b>
  </dt>
  <dd>
    <em>e.g.</em> The <u>Obvious benefits of</u> catalytic cracking are abundant
    feedstocks, low cost, mild operating conditions and low energy consumption.
  </dd>
  <dt>
    <b>+ Merit</b>
  </dt>
  <dd>
    <em>e.g.</em> <u>Another merit of</u> catalytic cracking is that it produces
    a higher proportion of branched alkanes, which burn more easily than
    straight-chain alkanes and are, therefore, an important component of petrol.
  </dd>
  <dt>
    <b>– Disadvantage</b>
  </dt>
  <dd>
    <em>e.g.</em> However, there are <u>various disadvantages of</u> catalytic
    cracking.
  </dd>
  <dt>
    <b>– Drawback</b>
  </dt>
  <dd>
    <em>e.g.</em> High feed velocity is <u>one of the drawbacks of</u> catalytic
    cracking.
  </dd>
  <dt>
    <b>– Downside</b>
  </dt>
  <dd>
    <em>e.g.</em> <u>Another downside of</u> catalytic cracking is low depth of
    processing.
  </dd>
</dl>
